Brazil

  • Andrea Bassitt
    Sao Paulo
    Danceficiente Group - Brazil
    rea at zip.net
    I do a mixedability work here. I studied contact with Tica Lemos and attended some mixedability workshops by Karen Peterson and Alito Alessi down here. Karen Peterson was here in September 1998 and I was invited to join and translate her workshop. After she left, the people who had been at the workshop invited to continue the work and I've done this for two years already. I work at a cultural state facility for people with disabilities name Lapa Station and I work with a total number of 40 participants, divided in 4 groups with different kinds of abilities. There is a more experimented group made of 12 people that is beginning to improve more artistically. We've performed for audiences a few times and we went to Fla-Bra DanceAble festival last week. Today there are four people who help me doing the work and we are interested in improving our skills to improve the work as well. I've kept in touch with Karen Peterson (she was back here last April), took some Skinner Releasing classes with Stephanie Skura and keep doing CI. As I'm graduated in Drama I didn't expect to go this far with mixedabilty work but once this has happened naturally I'd like to get as much information as possible about it. So I intend to do an intensive course in January or February in the US because that'll be vacation time here. If you have any suggestion let me know. And if you are interested I can tell you more about our work. [added 21 Nov 2000]

the BodyCartography Project
 is directed by Olive Bieringa and Otto Ramstad. Our ventures include dance performances, installation works, film/video projects and educational workshops. We investigate and create with the bodies' relationship to the physical, architectural, climatic, technological and social landscapes that we inhabit in urban/wilderness and private/public contexts. The Project is available for workshops, lecture demonstrations, performance and film projects. We blend training in: site-specific dance, Contact Improvisation, Body-Mind Centering, improvisational composition, integrated dance and dance video.

Dancing Wilderness Project
an ongoing investigation into the relationship amongst wilderness experience, body-based creative process, and how we live our lives, directed by Karl Frost. The Dancing Wilderness Project engages in performance work as well as in open labratories in the form of 2 to 3 week long wilderness backpacking trips which combine elements of dance workshop, open research, and open-ended wilderness experience. Wire Monkey Dance, Holyoke MA
Wire Monkey Dance Company’s innovative and compelling use of transforming sets of scaffolding along with surrounding video projections and live original music round out dynamic evening length multi-media dance events choreographed by Jennifer Polins and Saliq Francis Savage.
Wire Monkey Dance began creating dances on scaffolding in 1998. Since then, they have developed an innovative movement language in over 10 pieces on a variety of mobile configurations of construction scaffolding. They perform annually in Holyoke and Northampton, MA, and have toured in New England, Taiwan, China and Berlin.
