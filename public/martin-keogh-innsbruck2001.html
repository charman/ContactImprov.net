• In the Eye of the Hurricane •

Intensive Workshop (4 days) in Contact Improvisation

For those with a solid grasp of Contact fundamentals. Limited participants)

 

Amidst the velocity and whirling of a hurricane rests an area that is silent and still, known as the “eye.” Cultivating this quiet core at the center of activity is the theme of this workshop.
We will settle into a base camp of mindfulness, and then set out to investigate the wilderness of isorientation.  We will enter the forest where there's no trail, jump into the dark, swim into what might be a cave, and build a tolerance for waiting in the unknown.   Special emphasis on releasing the neck and pelvis, and surprising ourselves in flight and extended follow-through.

Biographie:
Martin Keogh has taught and performed Contact Improvisation for over 20 years. He has cofacilitated Contact Improvisation Teacher’s Conferences on four continents and was the first contact teacher on the faculty at Stanford University and at the National School of Fine Arts in San Miguel, Mexico. When not on tour, Martin dances with his family in Southeastern MA.
